      Radar Reflectors

      Phil

      Then it stands to reason that the Phantoms were using an active radar. Also aircraft based radars use a much more 'focussed beam' than do ground based surveillance radars for several reasons - they don't need the almost 90° vertical coverage required of ATC radars, and a focussed beam gets more power 'on the target'.

      Anyway this is all a diversion. ATC primary radars are high power, and work by detecting the very small amount of power reflected by aircraft - hence radar reflectors do work on primary radars. Secondary radars are 'interrogating' devices which require a coded response from an aircraft transponder - hence reflectors do not work for secondary.

      If we get to a state where all primary radars are switched off in preference for secondary radars, then no aircaft will be known to the ATC systems unless they are carrying an operational transponder.

      Alan

                Radar Reflectors

                "Good work gov.uk. you've just made the country more vulnerable to terrorist attack."

                Thought I'd chip in here! Where has the idea that primary radar is to be switched off come from? I work as an Air Traffic Engineer at Leeds Bradford Airport. Our primary is working fine and as far as I know there are no plans to discontinue the service.

                "Surely there must be some form of primary radar at least to cover sensitive areas if not the whole country"

                Indeed there is. The NATS long range radar stations such as Clee Hill, Claxby, Debden etc all have 23cm (1250MHz)
                primary radar systems as well as secondary. NATS are mid way through a programme to upgrade these systems (concludes in 2013 I think) so I doubt if they are also planning to turn them off.

                Most airfield radars are 10cm band (3000MHz) and ours (a Plessey Watchman, the most common type in the UK) can see microlights flying at Rufforth about 20 miles away withought any radar reflectors.

                Perhaps if some airfields have their radars off for maintenance it's because they need maintaining! And not some secret conspiracy to get rid of primary radar

                Cheers
                Martin

                            Radar Reflectors

                            Bob,

                            Away from anything metallic is good, so at least a foot or so from any spars. No, they have to be straight (or nearly so) - not wrapped around anything - an particularly anything conductive. If you have Raven battens, or any with a fibreglass section, you could wrap the tape legthwise around the fg rod (so you end up with a 108mm tube).

                            My wife came home from a garden centre with a roll of self-adhesive copper tape - meant for sticking around pots as a slug repellent. It was thinner copper than the RS type, but considerably cheaper. Probably just as good.

                            As Andy says, if you make a copper tape 'cross' at the base of your airband whip - each of the four legs leading out at right-angles, and each 22" long - it makes a pretty damned good groundplane. The centre of the cross connects to the aerial mounting hole and the coax outer.

                            Dave
